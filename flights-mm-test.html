Frequently Asked Questions

Q1.How long is a direct flight to Bangkok?

Direct flights to Bangkok from major Asian hubs typically take 2-4 hours. From Europe, direct flights range from 11-13 hours. From North America, expect 15-20 hours with a stopover.

Q2.What airlines fly to Thailand?

Major international airlines including Thai Airways, Singapore Airlines, Emirates, Qatar Airways, and Cathay Pacific serve Bangkok. Budget carriers like AirAsia and Nok Air also offer regional connections.

Q3.Do I need a visa on arrival?

Visa requirements depend on your nationality. Many countries receive 30-day visa exemption, while others may need to apply in advance. Check our visa guide for the latest requirements.

Q4.Which airport should I fly into?

Suvarnabhumi (BKK) handles most international flights and is connected to the city by Airport Rail Link. Don Mueang (DMK) serves budget airlines and domestic flights, also with good transport connections.

Q5.What is the best time to book flights?

For the best fares, book 2-3 months in advance for peak seasons (December-February). Shoulder seasons (March-May, September-November) often offer better prices and fewer crowds.
